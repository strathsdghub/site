About Us

The SDG Students Program is an initiative of SDSN Youth, the global youth division of the UN Sustainable Development Solutions Network (SDSN). SDSN Youthâ€™s mission is to empower young people globally to create sustainable solutions.
Through the SDG Students Program, SDSN Youth aims to engage students in higher education in the global effort to achieve the 2030 Agenda and the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), as well as empowering them with the knowledge, skills, and pathways to action to be effective agents of change today.

Why young people?
Young people make up nearly half the world's population - the biggest generation we are yet to see - they are the most educated, charitable, and entrepreneurial amongst other age groups. We believe that young people are the ideal agents to be at the forefront of the sustainable development movement: they are forward-thinking, creative, and come in large numbers.


         


SDSN Youth today
Thus, SDSN Youth was born to place young people at the forefront of achieving the SDGs. SDSN Youth was launched in 2015 by Professor Sachs - at the 5th meeting of the SDSN Leadership Council in Paris. Over the past five years, we have expanded rapidly, with over 3000 program members worldwide.

Theory of Change
We ensure that all university students of all walks of life can: LEARN about the SDGs, ENGAGE with and develop a passion for the SDGs, and ACT on implementing the SDGs in the community.

OUR MISSION

A global network of student hubs, where students can come together to learn about, engage with, and take action on the SDGs.

OUR VISION

To transform universities into hubs of learning and engagement for SDGs, as well as drivers of new solutions to local problems.

OUR OBJECTIVES

TRANSFORM

Universities across the world into hubs of learning and engagement into sustainability solutions and SDGs

&

CHALLENGE

Students to become agents of change, problem-solving, driving innovation and solutions to local modern problems for the new generation

SDGs & Me Campaign in collaboration with Vijana Tupo Initiative

We had a very intimate session where we shared our sentiments on the SDGs, the prospects of the youth in SDGs with Vijana Tupo Initiative, and the role of technology and the youth with the AFRICA DIGITAL ACADEMY.
It was insightful to have conversations with various youth in different spaces of action. We learned a lot and are proud of this campaign's continuous support and outcomes in the SDG space. If you want to be a part of this campaign, feel free to reach out and learn more about how you can be an SDG champion in your community!

  Admin   June 16, 2022
Transforming Education Summit

As the Strathmore SDG Hub, we got the privilege to host a grassroots consultation that is part of the #youthdeclaration consultation process in the lead-up to the Transforming Education Summit.
The consultation was facilitated by Doris Mwikali from the Sherpa Group UNESCO & Cynthia Nyongesa - Youth Leader at Global Partnership for Education, who shed more light on the essence of the #youthdeclaration. The youth declaration will help shape the agenda and the outcomes of the Transforming Education Summit in New York. This document will serve as an instrument for advocating for the needs of the young people and the necessary actions that must be taken to transform education and achieve the 5 action tracks of the Summit.
The students that joined us at the consultation had such meaningful insights. We are committed to ensuring that the youth's voices are heard at the national and global levels! We must play our role in #TransformingEducation!

Inaugural Climate Change Summit 2022

We were pleased to host the Inaugural Climate Change Summit at Strathmore University together with the Environmental and Sustainability Club, Strathmore Business School (SBS) and the Art Club. It was an opportunity for the youth to interact and inspire each other to innovate sustainable solutions aimed at protecting our one and only Earth ðŸŒŽ.
We had Eng. Millicent Alooh - HSC from Cancer Care Kenya, Nairobi (The MP Shah Hospitals) and Tomas Hurdsone - Founder and CEO of Youth Greenspace Action & Network as our Key Note Speakers. They shared about the remarkable work that their organizations are doing to fight and mitigate climate change.
One of the highlights of the Summit was watching the Journey to Justice Film from Konrad-Adenauer-Stiftung (Rule of Law Program for Anglophone Sub-Saharan Africa) which highlighted the journey to environmental justice in Kenya.
Furthermore, We were pleased to have Patrick Kiarie - Kenya Country Director for We Don't Have Time, and Esther Muiruri - Programs Officer for Disaster Risk Reduction, Climate Change Adaptation & Environment at Kenya Red Cross be on the panel about "Climate Governance- The Role of the Youth." They shared nuggets of wisdom on how the youth can work to involve themselves in fighting climate change at the local and international levels.
